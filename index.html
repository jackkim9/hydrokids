How do plants grow in soil versus water?

Soil naturally provides packets of water and air. It gives the roots structure so the plant can anchor itself securely. Nutrients come from rock and mineral leaching and rotting organic matter.

Why would you grow plants without soil?

In many locations, especially in cities, there simply isn't space or soil available to grow plants in the ground. You can explore growing food on board spacecraft. Hydroponic gardening can be used to help solve food shortages where the weather is bad. Hydroponics allows plants to grow at the fastest rate, reducing the time between planting and harvest.

How would you design a hydroponic system for a classroom?

This is a Deep Water Culture (DWC) hydroponic system.

  • The plants sit in little cups held above water.
  • Their roots hang down into the water to drink and get food.
  • A pump and bubbles add air to the water so the roots can breathe.
  • The plants grow in a soft stuff called rock wool.
  • A bright LED lamp gives the plants light to help them grow.
  • Makes it clean and easy to use in a classroom.

What problems might a farmer face when doing hydroponics?

Hydroponics can be expensive, require a continuous supply of electricity, be hard to maintain, and plants can catch waterborne diseases.

What happens if a plant doesn't get enough nutrients?

A hydroponic plant can have three nutrient deficiencies: nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. When it doesn't have enough nitrogen, it can turn yellow and its leaves may curl.
